Volunteers

Workawayers typically volunteer 4-5 hours a day in exchange for food and accommodation, with visits lasting anywhere from a few days to a few months.

Take a busman's holiday and put your existing skills to good use, or try something new you'd never normally get to do and pick up new skills along the way. Workaway is especially popular with travellers looking to immerse themselves in another language.

Travel solo or connect your profile to that of a partner or friend and travel together. There are currently over 9,000 active hosts in over 80 countries offering all kinds of places to stay.

Hosts

Whether you need support with a specific project, or another pair of hands on an ongoing basis, if you have extra room you can invite people to come and help.

Volunteers come from all sorts of backgrounds and bring with them a variety of abilities and skills. Typical tasks might involve helping with an eco-building project or general maintenance, child-minding and cooking, or admin work or designing a new website.

Workawayers work to help fund their travel, and you should expect to help them get the most out of the area when they visit. The better the welcome, the more you can expect in return.

why workaway?

  • Travel on a budget. Make the most of your $£€¥ and travel further for longer.
  • Travel deeper. Go beyond the guidebooks and experience local life first-hand with the people who live and work there.
  • Make a difference. Hosts range from individuals who need help to small organizations working for the benefit of their communities. The Workaway Foundation channels aid directly to educational and environmental projects in the developing world.
  • Do something different. Don't limit yourself to traditional tourist haunts, but take advantage of the workaway network to explore places and activities you'd barely even dreamed of until now.
  • New places, new faces. Workaway is a community. Everywhere you go there'll be someone waiting for you, and your hosts will give you a head start with what's going on locally. Using Workaway Meet Up you can find other travellers nearby to share your adventures with.
  • Be safe. Get to know hosts before you travel by reading their profiles, messaging them, and reading feedback from other volunteers. You can ask other workawayers about their experiences, and when staying with hosts learn from them about the local do's and don'ts.
